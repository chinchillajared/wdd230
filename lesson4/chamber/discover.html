Mount Chirripó

Mount Chirripó is the highest mountain in Costa Rica, with an elevation of 3,821 meters (12,536 feet). It is part of the Cordillera de Talamanca, and the range's highest point. It is located in Chirripó National Park and is noted for its ecological wealth.

Central Market

The Central Market is the largest market in the city of San José. Founded in 1880. It was declared a Cultural Heritage of Costa Rica for its strong tradition and identity.

National Theater

The National Theater is a building of historicist architecture completed in the year 1897. It is considered one of the most important buildings in national history, and the main architectural jewel of the city of San José.

San Isidro Labrador Parish

It is a religious temple of Catholic worship and parish under the invocation of San Isidro Labrador of the canton of Vázquez de Coronado, in Costa Rica. The existing temple was completed around 1935 in a neo-Gothic style.

San Jose

San José is the capital of the Republic of Costa Rica and the head of the homonymous province. Located in the center of the country, specifically on the intervolcanic plateau called Central Valley, and contained entirely by the homonymous canton.
